About DiabetesPredict

Our Mission

DiabetesPredict aims to use artificial intelligence to help identify individuals at risk of diabetes and provide them with actionable insights to prevent or manage the condition.

The Problem We're Addressing

Diabetes is a global health crisis affecting over 460 million people worldwide. Early detection and intervention can significantly reduce complications and improve quality of life. However, many cases go undiagnosed until serious symptoms appear.

Our AI-powered tool aims to bridge this gap by providing an accessible preliminary risk assessment that can motivate at-risk individuals to seek proper medical evaluation and make lifestyle changes.

Our AI Approach

DiabetesPredict uses machine learning, specifically a Random Forest classifier, to analyze health metrics and predict diabetes risk. The model is trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset, which contains health measurements from individuals along with their diabetes status.

Key aspects of our approach include:

  • Data preprocessing to handle missing values and normalize features
  • Feature importance analysis to identify the most significant risk factors
  • Probability calibration to provide meaningful risk percentages
  • Regular model evaluation and retraining to maintain accuracy

Key Features

  • Diabetes risk prediction
  • Feature importance analysis
  • Personalized health insights
  • Educational resources
  • API access for integration

Limitations

  • Not a diagnostic tool
  • Based on a specific population dataset
  • May not account for all risk factors
  • No substitute for medical advice
  • Requires accurate self-reported data

About the Dataset

The Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset originates from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases. It includes health measurements from females of Pima Indian heritage, at least 21 years old, with information about whether they developed diabetes within five years of these measurements.

The dataset includes features such as:

  • Number of pregnancies
  • Plasma glucose concentration
  • Diastolic blood pressure
  • Triceps skin fold thickness
  • 2-Hour serum insulin
  • Body mass index (BMI)
  • Diabetes pedigree function (genetic influence)
  • Age

Technical Implementation

Our application uses Flask for the web framework, with scikit-learn powering the machine learning components. The front-end is built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, using Bootstrap for responsive design and Chart.js for data visualization.

Machine Learning Pipeline:

  1. Data cleaning and preprocessing
  2. Feature scaling to normalize ranges
  3. Model training using Random Forest classifier
  4. Hyperparameter optimization via cross-validation
  5. Model evaluation on test data
  6. Feature importance extraction

The model achieves approximately 78% accuracy on the test dataset, with good sensitivity and specificity balance for diabetes prediction.
